About Us

In modern households, especially among busy individuals and families, it is common to have a variety of ingredients in the kitchen but struggle to decide what to cook with them. The problem is exacerbated by the lack of time to plan meals, leading to food waste or unhealthy eating habits. Meal planning apps can significantly alleviate this issue by providing tailored recipe suggestions based on the ingredients you have on hand. They help streamline the planning process, ensuring that meals are both nutritious and varied.


The primary users are busy individuals, students, and families who often find themselves with limited time and resources to plan and prepare meals. These users may lack the culinary creativity or knowledge to make the most of the ingredients they have.


Users experience frustration, food waste, and a reliance on unhealthy or expensive meal options when they cannot decide what to cook with the available ingredients. This impacts their time, budget, and overall well-being. To solve this problem, meal planning can help users create a meal plan with the ingredients they already have. It can also help users keep track of what they have already used and avoid food waste. Finally, meal planning apps can help users find healthy and affordable meal options.


    - The app will have a set of check boxes that the user will tick based on their available ingredients. The system will then proceed to the recipe generation step.
    - The app will suggest recipes that can be made with the entered ingredients, prioritizing those that require the least additional items.
    - The app will provide nutritional information for each recipe to promote healthy eating.
    - Users can filter recipes based on dietary restrictions, cuisine preferences, or time constraints.
    - If users are missing a few ingredients for a recipe, the app can generate a shopping list of the necessary items.
    - If users cannot choose what they want to cook, there will be a page where it shows the most common household ingredients that can be cooked and made into a meal.


The chosen pain point addresses a common challenge in daily life—deciding what to cook with the available ingredients. This problem is particularly relevant in today’s fast-paced environment where people seek convenience and efficiency in their daily routines. By reducing food waste, saving time, and promoting healthier eating, the app will provide significant value to users. It empowers them to make the most of what they have, enhancing their cooking experience and overall lifestyle.


-Paul Abellana, Francis Benedict Chavez, Moriel Bien, and Nina Margarette Catubig
